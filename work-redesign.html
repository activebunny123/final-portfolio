Responsive Redesign for Yan's Cuisine

Provide a responsive webdesign solution better suited for Yan's Cuisine

Background

Online ordering and food delivery has been growing fast as consumers enjoy the convenience, and COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated this trend. However, many restaurants struggle to provide a versatile online platform that balances between aesthetic, ease of navigation, and information.

This is the case with Yan's Cuisine, a Chinese restaurant located near Brown University's campus in Providence, RI. Though they have delicious food, their website is hard to navigate.

Challenge

I seek to redesign Yan's website so that it's responsive and can adapt to different devices. Also, I want to improve the layout of Yan's website such that it would be easier for customers to navigate. I will incorporate usability and accessibility principles to bring a clearer and more logical hierarchy and information flow to Yan's website, while keeping most of the information from its original site.

Problems

Based on my study of Yan's website, I have identified multiple usability and accessibility issues (using WAVE Website Accessibility Evaluation Tool), which are listed below.

Learnability

  • Unconventional login option on the left
  • Location & Hours separated by the logo picture
  • Standalone "form of payment"

Memorability

  • Too many menu categories have the same design
  • Unintuitive design: search bar & location Info at the bottom

Usability

  • Too much text and white space, takes too much time to scroll
  • Hard-to-find search bar & contact info at the bottom
  • Medium & Small Screen has overly small font

Accessibility

  • Very small text, expecially for menu categories
  • Redundant links causes confusion
  • Multiple labels associated with one input -- assistive tech unable to read the appropriate label

Design Choices

Layout for Larger Containers

The body is divided by grid, because the design is more static and follows a simple verticle flow regardless of screen size.

Each grid has the flex layout, because it is easier to arrange smaller elements and allow for responsive adjustments.

In-between Sizes

Most elements have units of "vw" or in percentage, which allows for automatic adjustment to change in screen size.

The breakpoint in screen size is chosen such that when elements do change size (Ex. h1 changes from 8 to 9 to 11vw for larger, medium, and small screen), the size increase wouldn't be too drastic.

Interactive components

The links in header, footer, and the buttons are interactive. The color changes on hover and click.

Reflection

Trade off Between Information and Clarity

During the design process, I have considered various choices regarding information preservation and the simplicity of the webpage. The original home page has all the menu items listed out on a side bar, which not only presents readability issues but is also unnecessary, as customers can always navigate to the menu page. Therefore, the redesign completely ditched the side-bar menu, and used a menu button at the most eye-catching position of the page instead.

I learned that webpage design should always consider what information is necessary, and what is redundant, and thus can be removd. This ensures the audience isn't bombarded by too many pieces of information at once, improving the ease of navigation and the overall experience.

Responsive Elements

In order to make the webpage responsive, I made two versions of the navigation bar (expanded and hamburger menu), and I used variable font sizes. One challenge I faced when designing the webpage was to make the transition of font size as smooth as possible when device dimension changes. It took many rounds of trial and error to figure out the right sizes. Even though the variable fonts worked for the majority of the content, the navigation bar became either too small or too crowded for the small screen size. Thus, I created a collapsable hamburger menu that replaces the navigation bar for smaller screens.

This experience taught me that UIUX design comes with repeated experimentation, and sometimes elements changes based on external variables (screen size in this case).
